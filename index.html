Bangladeshi culture is a vibrant, 2,500-year-old tapestry blending indigenous traditions, Islamic heritage, and Bengali language, heavily influenced by its riverine geography. Key elements include, the staple diet of rice and fish, colorful traditional clothing (Sari, Punjabi), rich folk music (Baul), and major festivals like Pohela Boishakh, Eid-ul-Fitr, and Durga Puja. It is a collective, family-oriented society emphasizing respect for elders and hospitality
